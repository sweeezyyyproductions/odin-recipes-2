Baked Ziti

Description

Baked ziti is a popular Italian-American dish that originated in Southern Italy. It is a hearty and comforting pasta dish that has become a staple in many American households. While its exact origins are unclear, baked ziti is believed to have been brought to the United States by Italian immigrants in the early 20th century. Over time, it has evolved into a beloved classic that is enjoyed by people of all backgrounds.

Baked ziti is not only delicious but also provides a good amount of protein, making it a satisfying and nourishing meal. The primary source of protein in this dish comes from the ground beef. Lean ground beef is commonly used, which offers a substantial amount of high-quality protein. Protein is essential for building and repairing tissues, supporting muscle growth, and maintaining a healthy immune system.

In addition to ground beef, baked ziti also incorporates cheese, such as ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan. Cheese is another excellent source of protein, particularly ricotta cheese, which is made from whey and contains a good amount of protein per serving. Mozzarella cheese, known for its stringy texture when melted, also contributes to the protein content of the dish. Parmesan cheese, with its rich and nutty flavor, adds a finishing touch and provides additional protein as well.

By combining the protein-rich ingredients of ground beef and cheese, baked ziti becomes a well-rounded and satisfying meal option. Protein not only helps to keep you full and satiated but also plays a crucial role in various bodily functions. Incorporating protein-rich dishes like baked ziti into your diet can contribute to a balanced and nutritious eating plan.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ziti pasta
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 jar (24 ounces) marinara sauce
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the ziti pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and cook until the onion is softened.
  4. Add the marinara sauce to the skillet and simmer for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix together the ricotta cheese, 1 cup of mozzarella cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped basil, chopped parsley, salt, and black pepper.
  6. In a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, layer half of the cooked ziti pasta. Spread the ricotta cheese mixture over the pasta evenly.
  7. Add the remaining ziti pasta on top of the cheese mixture. Pour the marinara sauce and beef mixture over the pasta, spreading it out evenly.
  8. Sprinkle the remaining 1 cup of mozzarella cheese on top of the sauce.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with additional chopped basil and parsley, if desired.
  11. Serve hot and enjoy!
